WW1 Red Summer

  • WW1 Red Summer

During WW1, it was the height of White Supremacy. White Americans were intent in ensuring that black people remembered their subordinate place in society and were willing to use violence to make that clear. In 1919, there were racial riots during the Summer in which blood was spilled - which is where the name Red Summer comes from.

Many riots in the past had been massacres, but this time black people took a stand. W.E.B Du Bois, goes to Europe to interview African-American troops, and learns a lot; He says "We Return from Fighting. We Return Fighting".
